Lehar Stool

Lehar is a simple stool inspired by the dynamic motion of waves and desert sand. The aim of the project was to design a stool to harness the beauty of wood bending while creating a functional stool that is not overly complicated to manufacture. Lehar stool is an example of how wood can be curved and organic.

Company:

Freelance Project


Duration:

1 Month

Role:

Furniture Designer

Tools Used:

Fusion 360, Keyshot, Figma

Mood board

The main inspiration behind the project were sand dunes in the desert. I wanted to capture the sense of transience that came with a dune. I also looked at rhythms, waves and materials that could inspire the form of the stool

Each dune holds the memory of a thousand winds.

Ideation

Sketching the stool was quite therapeutic, as it allowed me to be free with the form. I did have to keep the joineries in mind to ensure stability

Joinery

The trickiest part was figuring out how to keep the form sturdy, especially the curved backrest. The design was split into 5 pieces

Technical Drawings

After a lot of back and forth and tweaking, the dimensions of the stool were finalized

Prototyping

It was time to see if the stool could actually be manufactured. I started out with smaller models of the stool to check potential problems and stability

Building the Model

Bending the wood to match the right curve was a challenge and last minute adjustments had to be made

1:1 Prototype

Final Renders

Lehar stool is perfect to be placed in a home or an office for an unexpected visitor or for a quiet afternoon reading session by the window!
