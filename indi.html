Indi Pickle Vending Machine

Pickles (or Achar) are a very important condiment in Indian Cuisine that complete any meal. Indi is a concept of a vending machine that dispenses specialty pickles from all over India. From the classic raw mango to the exotic bamboo shoot, Indi has it all. This project is an experimental classroom project to explore Ui/Ux outside the traditional confines of mobile app and Desktop Website design

Researching what exists

In order to understand how vending machines work, I visited different vending machines and studied their interface to see what features I would have to include in mine

Existing Interface and Flow

Most of the machines studied followed a similar process. There was a screen to select the desired product, an option to add the selected items to the cart, a payment screen, a "dispensing in progress" screen, and finally, a confirmation screen showing that all the products had been dispensed.

Paper Sketches and Low fidelity Wireframes

The general aesthetics, flow and composition of the various frames were explored through simple sketches and explorations

Design System

The color palette was derived from the vibrant visual language of a traditional Indian condiment store, chosen to evoke cultural authenticity, stimulate visual engagement, and establish a distinctive brand identity

Branding and Illustrations

The bright colours of the fresh ingredients and delicious pickle were incorporated into custom illustrations made for the app. The logo also features a traditional Indian pickle jar to further tie in the cultural aspect of the project

Final UI

The app was designed to resemble a small pickle shop owned by a sweet Indian grandmother, evoking feelings of nostalgia, comfort, and warmth which are emotions closely

tied to pickles in many Indian households.

Animations

Animations were introduced to reduce perceived wait time and keep users engaged while products were being dispensed

High- Fidelity Prototype

Building the Form

I chose a simple, retro-inspired form for the vending machine to create a familiar, approachable aesthetic that resonates with users while keeping the design visually distinctive

Machine Exterior

Finally, it was time to give the kiosk a body to house the designed interface. This also contributed to the overall experience and flow of the kiosk, as it would be the one catching the users attentions and providing visual cues to guide them through the process of selecting, paying and collecting the pickles
