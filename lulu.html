Lulu Temporary Latch

Lulu is a temporary latch that doubles as a key chain and can be used if the user finds themselves in a situation where the latch of the door doesnt function


While many doors in public areas like, restrooms and washrooms, use latches as a locking mechanism, they can often be faulty due to lack of maintenance. This may cause users to not feel secure without a locked door. This project aims at providing a solution for such a situation in the form of a simple product

How Latches should work

I had never thought about latches until I encountered a faulty one when I was in a trial room of a clothing store. There were no other rooms so there was no where else I could go

My Experience

Project Brief

To create a product that can act as a temporary fastener for doors that do not have a functioning latch

Ideation

I tried to explore various locking mechanisms and how they could be attached to a door. It was important to keep the product as small and simple as possible, as I wanted the product to be portable

Low Fidelity Prototypes

Using simple materials like cardboard, ice cream sticks, M Seal, Rubber bands, tape and clips, I tried various combinations to see how the door could be effectively jammed.

Initial 3D models

Based on earlier ideations and feedback, I created simple 3D models to visualize how the latch might look

Prototyping

The concept was 3D printed and converted into key chains. This
helps the user carry the product around easily and have it on hand
if they happen to need it.

Final Testing

Seeing the latch in use was very satisfying, as it brought the design to life and demonstrated its practical application. This project was a great way to explore how even a simple mechanism can be both highly functional and useful.
